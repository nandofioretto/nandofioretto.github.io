PhD Position in ML and Optimization with application to Energy Systems

Location: Department of Computer Science, University of Virginia

Location: Department of Computer Science, University of Virginia, USA.

We are pleased to announce an exciting opportunity for a highly motivated PhD student to work on an NSF-funded project aimed at developing a physics-informed, real-time optimal power flow model using advanced machine learning techniques. This project has significant implications for the future of energy systems, contributing towards reduced carbon emissions, increased grid reliability, and widespread renewable energy integration.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Research and development of physics-informed machine learning models for real-time optimal power flow.
  • Collaboration in the development and application of techniques for ensuring constraint satisfaction and scalability of the learning algorithms.
  • Working closely with the project team to improve model robustness and reliability.
  • Actively engage in scientific communication through writing scientific papers for top-tier AI/ML/Energy conferences and journals.

Minimum Qualifications:

  • Master's degree in Computer Science, Physics, Electrical Engineering, or a closely related field.
  • Demonstrated experience in Machine Learning, Energy Systems, Physics, and/or Optimization.
  • Previous publications in top-tier AI/ML/Energy conferences or journals.
  • Strong programming skills, particularly in Python.
  • Excellent oral and written communication skills in English.

Desirable Qualifications:

  • Experience with Physics-Informed Neural Networks.
  • Familiarity with power systems and optimal power flow.
  • Knowledge of renewable energy systems.

What We Offer:

This is a unique opportunity to contribute to an innovative project that aims to revolutionize the energy sector. The successful candidate will have the chance to collaborate with national laboratories and non-profit organizations, and impact utilities, policymakers, and researchers' work. In addition, the project offers a great training opportunity in the intersection of physics, engineering, and machine learning, thereby contributing to the development of a skilled workforce in the field of energy and sustainability.

University of Virginia is an equal opportunity employer and encourages applications from all qualified individuals.

To Apply

Applications should be submitted at fi...nia.edu. Interested candidates are requested to submit a detailed CV, a motivation letter, and the names and contact details of two referees. The position will remain open until filled, and the review of applications will begin immediately.

Informal inquiries are also welcome and may be sent to fi...nia.edu.
